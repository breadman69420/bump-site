child safety standards

last updated: april 5, 2026

bump has a zero-tolerance policy toward child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) and toward any use of the app that endangers minors. this page documents the standards we hold ourselves to, how we prevent CSAE on our platform, and how to report concerns.

1. who bump is for

bump is designed exclusively for adults 18 years of age or older. the app's target age group in the google play store is set to 18+. bump is not marketed to, designed for, or tested with minors. we do not offer child-directed features, kid-mode, or parental controls, because the app is not intended for use by children at any point.

2. what bump is (and what bump is not)

bump is a peer-to-peer contact-exchange utility. two users who are physically next to each other can swap a phone number, an instagram handle, a linkedin, or a snapchat over bluetooth low energy. received information displays for fifteen seconds and then self-destructs from device memory. nothing about the exchange ever reaches our servers.

bump does not:

  • host, store, transmit, or relay any photos, videos, audio, or other media files
  • provide any user profile, user-generated content feed, or discovery surface
  • allow users to browse, search, or follow other users
  • enable strangers at a distance to contact each other — physical proximity is required for every interaction
  • include chat, messaging, voice calling, or video calling
  • retain any content of a contact exchange beyond 15 seconds on the receiving device

because bump has no media channel and no content feed, there is no technical surface inside the app on which child sexual abuse material (CSAM) could exist, be uploaded, be viewed, or be shared.

3. how we prevent CSAE on our platform

  • no media channel: the app cannot be used to send images, videos, or any non-text content between users. there is no path for CSAM to travel through bump.
  • no strangers at a distance: bump requires both users to be physically adjacent and to both deliberately initiate a session. a predator cannot remotely reach, follow, groom, or target a user of bump.
  • strict 18+ age policy: use of the app by persons under 18 violates our terms and results in permanent removal via the device blocklist.
  • abuse reporting built into every session: the end of every completed session offers a "report" action. reports are triaged by our team.
  • auto-blocklist: devices confirmed to have misused the app — including any attempt to use it in a way that endangers minors — are added to an auto-blocklist and can no longer initiate sessions.
  • rate limiting: per-reporter and per-device rate limits prevent both harassment-by-report and pattern abuse by the same actor across multiple sessions.

4. how to report CSAE concerns

if you believe bump has been used to facilitate child sexual abuse or exploitation, or if you have any concern about content or behavior you encountered through the app, report it immediately through any of these channels:

  • in-app: tap report at the end of any session
  • email: bumpnowapp@gmail.com with subject line "CSAE report"

we commit to acknowledging every CSAE report within 24 hours of receipt and taking appropriate action — including device blocklisting, law-enforcement referral, and account-level enforcement where applicable.

for users in the United States, you can also report suspected CSAE directly to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) CyberTipline at report.cybertip.org or 1-800-843-5678. for users outside the US, report to the child-safety authority in your country (e.g., the Internet Watch Foundation in the UK, the RCMP NCECC in Canada).

5. law enforcement cooperation

bump complies with all applicable laws regarding the reporting and handling of child sexual abuse material and child exploitation, including (in the United States) 18 U.S.C. § 2258A. we cooperate fully with lawful requests from US and international law-enforcement agencies investigating CSAE.

when compelled by a valid legal request, we can provide the limited data we hold: anonymous one-way device hashes, session timestamps, and abuse-report records. we cannot provide user identities because we do not collect them, and we cannot provide exchange contents because they never reach our servers.

6. our commitment

bump commits to:

  • maintaining an app architecture that has no surface on which CSAM can exist
  • reviewing every CSAE-flagged report within 24 hours
  • permanently blocklisting any device confirmed to have used the app in a way that endangers a minor
  • reporting suspected CSAE to NCMEC CyberTipline where required by US law
  • cooperating with lawful investigations by qualified child-safety authorities
  • reviewing and updating these standards at least annually
  • not using user data for any purpose inconsistent with the promises made here and in our privacy policy

7. contact

questions, concerns, or reports about these standards — or about anything else related to child safety on bump — can be sent to bumpnowapp@gmail.com.

for general privacy questions, see our privacy policy.
